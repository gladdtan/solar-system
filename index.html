• Sun

    A massive, glowing ball of hot gases at the center of our solar system. It provides the energy and light that make life on Earth possible and holds the entire solar system together with its gravity.

  • Mercury

    The smallest and closest planet to the Sun. It has a rocky surface covered in craters, no atmosphere to speak of, and experiences extreme temperature changes between day and night.

  • Venus

    Similar in size and structure to Earth but with a thick, toxic atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide. Its surface is extremely hot, hotter than Mercury, due to a runaway greenhouse effect.

  • Earth

    The third planet from the Sun and the only known world to support life. It has a breathable atmosphere, liquid water on the surface, and a protective magnetic field.

  • Mars

    Known as the Red Planet because of its iron-rich dust. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system and shows evidence that liquid water once flowed on its surface.

  • Jupiter

    The largest planet, a gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium. It has a giant storm called the Great Red Spot and more than 90 moons, including Ganymede, the biggest moon in the solar system.

  • Saturn

    A gas giant famous for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock. It has dozens of moons, including Titan, which has a thick atmosphere and liquid lakes of methane.

  • Uranus

    An ice giant with a pale blue-green color due to methane gas. It rotates on its side, making its seasons extreme and unusual. It's cold and distant, with faint rings and many moons.
